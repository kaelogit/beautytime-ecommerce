Adapting Your Skincare to Nigerian Weather

Published on November 25, 2025 | Seasonal Care

Skincare in Nigeria is a constant battle against extreme humidity and the dry, dusty Harmattan winds. Your routine needs to be flexible to prevent both acne and dryness.

1. The Humid Rainy Season (Acne & Oil Control)

During peak humidity, your skin overproduces oil (sebum). This is when products feel heavy, and breakouts are common.

  • **Cleanse Twice Daily:** Use a foaming or gel cleanser (like a Face Wash) to prevent pore congestion.
  • **Light Hydration:** Switch to oil-free, gel-based moisturizers. Skip thick creams.
  • **Use Salicylic Acid:** Introduce a BHA product (like a Toner or Serum) two to three times a week to keep pores clear.

2. The Dry Harmattan Season (Barrier Repair)

The Harmattan brings extremely dry air, causing skin irritation, peeling, and barrier damage.

  • **Switch to Cream Cleansers:** Use gentler, hydrating cleansers to avoid stripping moisture.
  • **Layer Hydration:** Apply a hydrating serum (like Hyaluronic Acid) under a thicker Face Cream or Body Cream to lock in moisture.
  • **Protect Lips & Hands:** Carry Hand Creams and use a thick lip balm constantly.
